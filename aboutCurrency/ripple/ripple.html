Ripple

Ripple, launched in 2012, is a digital payment protocol designed to facilitate fast, low-cost, and secure cross-border transactions. Unlike traditional blockchains, Ripple uses a consensus ledger called the XRP Ledger (XRPL) and its native cryptocurrency, XRP, to enable liquidity and instant transfers. Ripple's primary focus is on transforming global payments by partnering with financial institutions, providing them with tools to settle transactions in real-time. Known for its energy efficiency and scalability, Ripple has gained prominence in the financial industry as a bridge between traditional banking systems and decentralized digital assets, streamlining international payments with reduced costs and delays.
