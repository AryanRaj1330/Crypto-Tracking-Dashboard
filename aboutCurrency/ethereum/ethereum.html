Ethereum

Ethereum is a decentralized blockchain platform launched in 2015 by Vitalik Buterin, designed to support smart contracts and decentralized applications (dApps). Unlike Bitcoin, Ethereum enables developers to create programmable transactions, offering various use cases beyond value transfer. Its native cryptocurrency, Ether (ETH), powers the network, facilitating transactions and rewarding validators. Ethereum’s key innovation is smart contracts—self-executing agreements that automatically perform actions when conditions are met, reducing the need for intermediaries. Ethereum also supports decentralized finance (DeFi), enabling users to borrow, lend, and trade without traditional institutions. It is central to the creation of non-fungible tokens (NFTs), which represent unique digital assets. To improve scalability and energy efficiency, Ethereum is transitioning to a proof-of-stake (PoS) model through Ethereum 2.0. This upgrade aims to boost network speed, reduce energy consumption, and enhance security. Ethereum’s adaptability, developer community, and widespread adoption make it a leader in blockchain technology, fostering innovation and competition in the crypto space.
